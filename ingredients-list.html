    A

    AHA: Also known as Alfa Hydroxy Acid. It function is to exfoliate the skin by removing dead cells. are acids derived from natural substances such as sugar cane, milk, and grapes.
    They work by melting the intercellular glue that holds our skin cells together, to help the skin’s natural shedding process. AHA’s are made up of tiny molecules of water loving and water soluble acids. AHAs are preferred for sun-damaged and dry skin because they exfoliate the skin’s surface and have the added benefit of also improving moisture content. No matter which AHA you choose, the strong exfoliating effects make your skin more sensitive to the sun. Wear sunscreen every morning to prevent burns, age spots, and increased skin cancer risks.

    B

    BHA: Beta-hydroxy acids, is a oil-soluble exfoliant. It is a nonabrasive acid derived from salicin, a chemical similar in nature to aspirin. Salicin is primarily extracted from willow bark, but may also originate from wintergreen and sweet birch. BHA posses the ability to loosen and break the attachment between cells. This process causes the formation of new cells while exfoliating the epidermis, or outer layer of skin. It can get deep into the pores and remove dead skin cells and excess sebum, to couter the formation of comedones.
    Although BHAs don’t make your skin as sensitive to the sun compared to AHAs, you should still wear sunscreen every single day. Currently, the BHA most commonly used in cosmetics is salicylic acid. On rare occasions, citric acid is also cited as a BHA in cosmetic formulations.

    E

    Edetate Disodium: also marked as Disodium EDTA or Disodium dihydrogen. Is a chemical used for both industrial and medical purposes It is used as a preservative, to stabilise it, or to enhance the foaming action. It doesn’t absorb particularly well into the skin and it is considered safe.

    F

    Ferments: also known as probiotics, refers to skin care products loaded with good bacteria, usually from fermented natural ingredients. Fermentation is a natural proccess when a microorganism (usually a bacteria) is added to an organic material. The bacteria releases enzymes that break down the natural ingredient and changes its molecular structure. Many studies have proved that fermented ingredients are more potent as well as more effective for sensitive and dry skin. In the other hand, some people can suffer from allergies and worsed skin conditions when using fermented ingredients.

    Guaiazulene Guaiazulene is a dark blue crystalline hydrocarbon and derivative of azulene, the blue colored constituent of chamomile oil. It is used to soothe and calm the skin. It does not have any known side effects.

    Glicolic Acid. Belonging to the AHA family, it is obtained from sugar cane. It is used in concentration between 4% and 20%. The lower percentages are used for light exfoliations, while higher concentrations are used for despigmentation, wrinkles or acne treatments. It is recommended to use it a maximum of 2 times a week, otherwise it can cause irritation.
